\begin{frame}{Research question} \label{rq}
    \metroset{block = fill}
    How does the average treatment intensity around a physician's medical school impact their current practice intensity post-training?

    \vspace{5mm}
    \begin{block}{Treatment intensity}
        For a given year, 
            \begin{equation*}
                (\text{intensity}) = \frac{\text{number of surgeries}}{\text{number of unique patients}}.
            \end{equation*}
        \hyperlink{intensity}{\beamerbutton{Intensity specifics}}
    \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Previous literature}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Variation in physician practice style exists despite medical standards
            \begin{itemize}
                \item Evidenced by geographic variation \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{finkelstein_sources_2016}}
                \item Drives differences in healthcare utilization \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{badinski_geographic_2023}, \textcite{cutler_physician_2019}}
            \end{itemize}
        
        \vspace{2.5mm}
        \item Many factors influence practice style once a physician begins practicing 
            \begin{itemize}
                \item Financial incentives \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{clemens_physicians_2014}}
                \item New research \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{decicca_how_2024}}
                \item Peers and practice environment \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{epstein_formation_2009}, \textcite{molitor_evolution_2018}}
            \end{itemize}

        \vspace{2.5mm}
        \item But there's limited evidence on the impact of formal training
            \begin{itemize}
                \item Variance decomposition \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{epstein_formation_2009}}
                \item Ranking \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{doyle_returns_2010}, \textcite{schnell_addressing_2018}}
                \item Interaction with racial disparities \textcolor{gray}{\scriptsize\textcite{kissel_role_2025}}
            \end{itemize}
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Contribution}
    Current literature is unable to comment how the knowledge gained in medical school shapes a physician's current practice.
    \begin{itemize} 
        \item[$\to$] Create a measure of treatment intensity in medical school market that quantifies the ``standard of care" taught in medical school
        \item[$\to$] Assess how similarly a surgeon practices compared to their alma mater market
    \end{itemize}

    
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Empirical strategy: Level of intensity}
    \metroset{block=fill}

    In year $t$, for surgeon $i$ who graduated medical school in year $t_g$,   
        \begin{align*}
            intensity_{it} = 
            &\beta(mover_i \times \textit{med school HRR intensity}_{it_g}) \\
            &+ \psi\textit{med school HRR intensity}_{it_g} + \theta X_i + \eta M_{it} \\
            &+ \{\textit{current HRR FEs}\}_i + \{year FEs\}_t + \epsilon_{it},
        \end{align*}
    where
        \begin{itemize}
            \item $X_i$ includes physician characteristics: gender, graduation year, subspecialty
            \item $M_{it}$ includes physician's Medicare patient population characteristics: age, race, gender, dual-enrollment, risk score
        \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Empirical strategy: Change in intensity}
    \metroset{block=fill}

    In year $t$, for surgeon $i$ who graduated medical school in year $t_g$,   
        \begin{align*}
            intensity_{it} = 
            &\beta(mover_i \times \textit{change in intensity}_{it,t_g}) \\
            &+ \psi\textit{med school HRR intensity}_{it_g} + \theta X_i + \eta M_{it} \\
            &+ \{\textit{current HRR FEs}\}_i + \{year FEs\}_t + \epsilon_{it},
        \end{align*}
    where
        \begin{itemize}
            \item $\textit{change in intensity}_{it, t_g} = \textit{current HRR intensity}_{it}
            - \textit{med school HRR intensity}_{it_g}$
            \item $X_i$ includes physician characteristics: gender, graduation year, subspecialty
            \item $M_{it}$ includes physician's Medicare patient population characteristics: age, race, gender, dual-enrollment, risk score
        \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Threats to validity}
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Differential selection into moving 
            \begin{itemize}
                \item[$\to$] Control for observable physician characteristics
            \end{itemize}

        \vspace{2.5mm}
        \item Movers only go to markets with intensity similar to their medical school market
            \begin{itemize}
                \item[$\to$] Distribution of mover differences between med school and destination markets is more spread out than stayers 
                \item[$\to$] Within movers, med school intensity and destination intensity is not correlated
            \end{itemize}
    \end{enumerate}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Conclusion}
    Differential exposure to treatment intensity in a surgeon's medical market does not have an effect on a their current practice. 
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Information conveyed about surgery is relatively standard across all medical schools. 
            \item Medical school establishes a foundational knowledge base, but practical knowledge is developed in postgraduate training. 
        \end{itemize}

    \vspace{2.5mm}
    \textbf{Where this is headed}
        \begin{itemize}
            \item Less noisy measures of treatment intensity using Medicare claims data 
            \item Less noisy measure of exposure during medical school using rotation hospital sites 
            \item Inclusion of residency
        \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Treatment intensity measure} \label{intensity}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Based on code description, I categorize all CPT codes billed to a surgeon as surgery, diagnostic procedure, office visit, or ``dependent" service. 
        \item All pathology and lab codes are assigned as diagnosis. 
        \item All patient-facing or patient-interaction codes are assigned office visit. 
        \item Dependent services include procedures that are not stand-alone and often billed together with surgery, e.g. anesthesia. 
    \end{itemize}
